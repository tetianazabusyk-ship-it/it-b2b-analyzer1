Schedule a CleverStaff walkthrough with Tetiana Zabusyk
CleverStaff is your main ATS — where all candidates, vacancies, pipelines, and feedback live. Schedule a working session directly with Tetiana Zabusyk (Head of Recruitment) to get a full walkthrough. During the same meeting, bring all the questions you've accumulated from Day 1 & Day 2. Learn: how to create a vacancy, add candidates, fill scorecards, log feedback, and track pipeline stages. Every candidate interaction must be logged here — no exceptions.

Study the Topgrading / WHO Interview System
Topgrading is the core structured interview methodology used at YozmaTech. Read the guide — understand the WHO framework, chronological work history method, and the key question categories. This system helps you identify A-players consistently. Study the overview, then review the full 50-competency framework and key question blocks.
🕐 ~1.5 hoursPriority: HIGHMethodology💬 WHO Interview Guide →🎯 Full Topgrading →+60 XP
Learn BlueDot & practice writing interview feedback
BlueDot records and transcribes your interviews automatically. Your job is to write structured feedback after every call. Key rule: EVERY feedback — positive OR negative — must be logged in CleverStaff. No feedback = candidate cannot move forward (Golden Rule #2). Practice writing a mock feedback using the template provided.
🕐 ~1 hourPriority: HIGHBlueDot🔵 Feedback Guide →+55 XP
Learn the Tech Interview Introduction Script
As a recruiter, you open every tech interview by introducing yourself, the candidate, and the interviewer. Read the official guide and memorise the script structure. Practice it out loud — it needs to sound natural and professional, not robotic. Your first impression sets the tone for the whole interview.
🕐 ~30 minInterview Skills🤝 Tech Intro Script →+45 XP
Master the Candidate Summary — read guide & practice writing one
The candidate summary is what the client reads first. It must be clear, concise, and compelling. Read the full instructions guide and the template. This is a core daily deliverable — you'll write these for every candidate you send to a client.

Tasks
Learn & practice the Reference Check process
Reference checks are mandatory before every offer. Read the full instructions: copy the template, rename it with the candidate's name, remove irrelevant questions, add any client-specific questions, conduct the check with the candidate's most recent direct manager, finalise the document, and upload it to CleverStaff. Also review the actual Reference Check Template in the KB to understand what questions to ask and how to document scores.
🕐 ~1.5 hoursPriority: HIGH📋 Ref Check Instructions →🔍 Ref Check Template →+55 XP
Master the Offer & Onboarding flow — end to end
Read the full "Sending an Offer and Onboarding a Candidate" guide. Understand the 6-step process: open & edit offer doc → convert to PDF → send offer + contract via email template → update CRM status → send Contract Form to candidate (UA GIG / B2B FOP / UA FOP/B2B) → create Jira card. Know which contract form applies to which candidate type. If candidate has no FOP — direct them to legal advisor @Maks_Amigo on Telegram. Also read the Job Offer Letter template to understand what the candidate receives.
🕐 ~1.5 hoursPriority: HIGH📬 Offer & Onboarding Guide →📄 Job Offer Letter →+60 XP
Learn Jira — People Management & pre-onboarding card
After offer acceptance, the recruiter opens a Jira card in the People Management (Onboarding & Offboarding) section and fills in all required pre-onboarding information. Study the Jira manual to understand every field: individual tax number, IBAN/SWIFT, equipment needs, contract type (GIG/FOP), English level, timezone, and Ukraine-specific info (backup power, internet during blackouts). For Ukraine employees — always add a comment about backup power sources (minimum 2 full working days) and internet availability.
🕐 ~1 hourPriority: HIGH🗂️ Jira Manual →+55 XP
Learn the Canva CV template & practice creating a candidate profile
YozmaTech uses a branded Canva template for all candidate CVs submitted to clients. Open the template, explore how it's structured, and practice creating a sample CV for a fictional candidate. The CV must always be sent in the corporate format — never a raw PDF or LinkedIn export. This is part of your daily workflow when presenting candidates.
🕐 ~1 hourBranding🎨 Open Canva Template →+45 XP
Explore the CRM templates & sourcing spreadsheet
Review the Google Drive folder with CRM templates and the sourcing tracking spreadsheet. Understand how vacancy pipelines are structured, how candidate data is tracked, and how weekly reports are generated. Familiarise yourself with the standard formats used by the team — consistency matters for handoffs and client reporting.
🕐 ~45 minTools📊 Sourcing Spreadsheet →📁 Templates Folder →+45 XP
Review candidate summaries doc & prepare your first draft

Tasks
Create your first vacancy on the YozmaTech website
Using a real or practice vacancy brief from your manager, create the full vacancy in CleverStaff CRM and publish it to the company website. Follow the 24-hour rule — vacancy must be live within 24 hours of kickoff. Fill in all mandatory blocks: salary range, client/project description, interview stages and duration, requirements, and responsibilities. Use the approved internal formatting. Then assign a Hot Vacancy status and start the active sourcing process.
🕐 ~1.5 hoursPriority: HIGH📋 Recruitment Policy →✅ Recruiter Responsibilities →+60 XP
Schedule intro meeting with Yulia Chaika — learn Wendify
Wendify is one of the core tools in your recruiter stack. Schedule an intro meeting with Yulia Chaika (your Wendify point of contact) to learn the platform: how it integrates with your sourcing pipeline, how to use it for candidate tracking, and what automation features are available. Come to the meeting with prepared questions. After the session, log your key takeaways in your onboarding notes.
🕐 ~1 hourTools🛠️ Recruiter PRO Tools →+45 XP
Create your first candidate CV in CVMaker
Using CVMaker (one of the approved recruiter tools), build a complete candidate CV in the YozmaTech corporate format. Use a real candidate from your pipeline or a practice profile provided by your manager. The CV must include: candidate's name & photo, role, English level, location, key skills with years of experience, brief professional summary, and work history highlights. This is the document you will send to the client alongside the candidate summary. Compare the result with the Canva template you learned on Day 3.
🕐 ~1 hourTemplates🛠️ CVMaker →📝 Summary Guide →+50 XP
Master the Pre-Offer Playbook — scripts, risk assessment & strong positioning
This is the most critical skill of the week. Read both playbooks in the KB carefully: the Pre-Offer Confirmation Call algorithm (6 steps + risk matrix) and the Job Offer Process — Strong Position guide. Understand the difference between weak and strong phrasing. Memorise the Roma Method confirmation script, the Marina Method urgency script, and the If-Then negotiation technique. Practice out loud — write 3 of your own strong-phrasing questions and compare them to the weak versions. Know exactly when to escalate to the CSM.

Tasks
Study the Recruiter–Sourcer Collaboration Policy
Read the full Recruiter & Sourcer Collaboration Policy (v1.1). Understand the core principle: the Recruiter is the sole owner of the vacancy and bears full responsibility for results. Know your Rights: Right of Veto over any candidate, Right to reject a profile without interview if quality standards aren't met. Understand the 5 mandatory data points required from a Sourcer for every candidate: notice period, salary (gross, USD), location, English level, full CV with tech stack. Learn the Zero Results meeting trigger — when and how it happens.
🕐 ~1 hourPriority: HIGH📋 Collaboration Policy →+50 XP
Schedule KPI meeting with Head of Recruitment — prepare your questions
Schedule a dedicated meeting with the Head of Recruitment (Nazar Stasiuk) to align on your KPIs for the probationary period (3 months). Prepare your questions before the meeting — the goal is to leave with full clarity on: expected number of hires/month, time-to-hire target, time-to-fill target, pipeline quality metrics, bonus structure triggers, and what success looks like at 30/60/90 days. Write your questions down — no question is too basic at this stage.
🕐 ~1.5 hoursPriority: CRITICAL📊 KPI & Metrics →🗺️ Onboarding Plan →+60 XP
Set up monthly 1:1 meetings with Head of Recruitment
After the KPI meeting, set up a recurring monthly 1:1 session with Nazar Stasiuk in the calendar. These monthly check-ins are your primary feedback loop — use them to review progress against KPIs, raise blockers, discuss pipeline health, and align on priorities for the next month. Monthly cadence = monthly accountability. Don't wait for problems to escalate — use the 1:1 proactively.
🕐 ~15 minRecurring+40 XP
Document your trial KPIs & write your 30/60/90-day plan
Based on the KPI meeting, document your personal targets for the probationary period in writing: close X positions per quarter, handle 3–5 active vacancies simultaneously, deliver first candidate within 3 days of vacancy opening, reach 85% quality of hire. Write a brief 30/60/90-day plan: what you'll focus on in Month 1 (learning, first hires), Month 2 (execution, full pipeline), Month 3 (results, process improvements). Share with your manager for approval.
